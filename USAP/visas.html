Visas

Visas are official documents issued by the government of a country, allowing an individual to enter, stay, or work within that country for a specified time and for specific purposes. Different types of visas are issued based on the purpose of travel and the duration of stay. Here are some common types of visas:

Eligibility: Temporary visitors for business purposes.

Application Process: Complete Form DS-160, attend an interview, provide evidence of the purpose of the business visit.

Eligibility: Temporary visitors for tourism or medical treatment.

Application Process: Complete Form DS-160, attend an interview, prove ties to the home country.

Eligibility: Fiancé(e)s of U.S. citizens planning to get married in the U.S.

Application Process: U.S. citizen files Form I-129F, undergo background checks, attend an interview.

Eligibility: Spouses and minor children of the U.S. Green Card holders.

Application Process: U.S. Green Card holder files Form I-130, wait for the priority date, complete Form DS-260, attend an interview.

Eligibility: Individuals coming to the U.S. for training not available in their home country.

Application Process: File Form I-129, provide details of the training program, get approval.

Eligibility: Job offer requiring specialized knowledge and a related degree or work experience.

Application Process: Obtain Labor Condition Application (LCA), file Form I-129, go through lottery selection (if applicable), get approval.

Eligibility: Employees of multinational companies transferring to a U.S. office.

Application Process: File Form I-129, provide proof of relationship, get approval.

Eligibility: Nationals of treaty countries investing in a U.S. business.

Application Process: Make substantial investment, complete Form DS-160, attend an interview, get approval.

Eligibility: Individuals with extraordinary ability in various fields.

Application Process: File Form I-129, provide substantial evidence of extraordinary ability, get approval.

Eligibility: Canadian and Mexican professionals in specific occupations.

Application Process: Obtain a job offer, provide necessary documents, apply at a U.S. port of entry, get approval.

Eligibility: Foreign investors making a qualifying investment in a U.S. business.

Application Process: Make qualifying investment, file Form I-526, receive conditional Green Card, file Form I-829 to remove conditions.

Eligibility: Temporary non-agricultural workers with job offers.

Application Process: Employer demonstrates need, file Form I-129, get approval.

Eligibility: Participants in approved exchange programs.

Application Process: Obtain sponsorship, receive Form DS-2019, attend an interview, get approval.

Eligibility: Religious workers, including ministers and religious professionals.

Application Process: File Form I-129, provide evidence of religious work and sponsorship, get approval.

Eligibility: International students enrolled in academic or vocational programs.

Application Process: Get accepted into a SEVIS-approved school, complete Form DS-160, attend an interview, get approval.
