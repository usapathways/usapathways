Green Card

Officially known as a Permanent Resident Card, is an immigration document that allows a foreign national to live and work in the United States permanently. It grants the holder the status of a lawful permanent resident, which means they have the right to reside and work in the U.S. indefinitely. Green Card holders also have certain rights and responsibilities, including the ability to help their immediate family members obtain Green Cards and, when specific conditions are met, the option to pursue U.S. citizenship.

These are available to immediate relatives of U.S. citizens (spouses, unmarried children under 21, and parents of U.S. citizens) as well as certain relatives in preference categories (such as unmarried children over 21 and siblings of U.S. citizens).

These are available to foreign nationals who have been sponsored by a U.S. employer based on their job skills and qualifications. Employment-based Green Cards have different preference categories, with higher categories generally requiring more advanced qualifications or job skills.

The Diversity Visa Lottery is a program that randomly selects individuals from countries with low rates of immigration to the U.S. Winners of the lottery have the opportunity to apply for a Green Card.
