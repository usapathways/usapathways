Eligibility
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
Be a permanent resident (Green Card holder). Have lived in the U.S. for at least 5 years (or 3 years if married to a U.S. citizen). Meet other eligibility criteria, such as good moral character and physical presence requirements.
↓
Form N-400
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
File Form N-400, the Application for Naturalization, with the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).
↓
Biometrics Appointment
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
Attend a biometrics appointment to provide fingerprints, photograph, and signature.
↓
Interview Notice
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
Receive a notice for a naturalization interview.
↓
Naturalization Interview
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
Attend an interview with a USCIS officer. Answer questions about your application and take the English and civics tests (unless exempt due to age or disability).
Tip: Study for the English and civics tests, which cover U.S. history and government.
↓
Oath of Allegiance
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
If approved, you will receive an Oath of Allegiance ceremony date. Take the Oath of Allegiance to become a U.S. citizen.
↓
Ceetificate of Naturalization
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
After taking the Oath, you will receive a Certificate of Naturalization, proving your U.S. citizenship.
↓
Update
Time: Usually several years (varies by country)
Attend an interview with a USCIS officer. Update your Social Security record and other government records to reflect your new citizenship status.
