Refugee

Eligibility: Individuals must be outside their home country and unable to return due to a well-founded fear of persecution based on the same criteria as asylum.

Application Process:
  • Referral: Typically, refugees are referred by the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) or other authorized organizations.

  • Interview: Applicants go through an interview process with officials from U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) or, in some cases, with the U.S. Department of State.

  • Security Checks: Security checks are conducted to ensure the applicant does not pose a security threat to the U.S.

  • Medical Examination: A required medical examination is conducted to identify any health concerns.

  • Resettlement: If approved, the individual is resettled in the U.S. and receives assistance from local refugee resettlement agencies.

Eligibility: Victims of severe forms of human trafficking in the U.S., such as forced labor or commercial sexual exploitation.

Application Process:
  • Law Enforcement Certification: Obtain a certification from a law enforcement agency confirming the victim's cooperation in the investigation or prosecution of trafficking.

  • Form I-914: File Form I-914, Application for T Nonimmigrant Status, with USCIS, along with necessary supporting documents.

  • Wait for a Decision: USCIS reviews the application. If approved, the T Visa allows the victim to stay and work in the U.S. for up to four years.

Eligibility: Individuals must prove they have suffered persecution or have a well-founded fear of persecution in their home country based on race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group.

Application Process:
  • File Form I-589: Complete and submit Form I-589, Application for Asylum and for Withholding of Removal, to the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) within one year of arrival in the U.S.

  • Biometrics and Interview: Attend a biometrics appointment and an interview with an asylum officer to present your case and provide supporting evidence.

  • Wait for a Decision: USCIS will decide on the asylum application. If approved, the individual can stay in the U.S. and apply for a Green Card after one year.

Eligibility: Victims of qualifying crimes, including domestic violence, sexual assault, and human trafficking, who have suffered substantial mental or physical abuse and are willing to assist law enforcement.

Application Process:
  • Law Enforcement Certification: Obtain certification from law enforcement confirming the victim's helpfulness in the investigation or prosecution of the crime.

  • Form I-918: File Form I-918, Petition for U Nonimmigrant Status, along with the law enforcement certification and supporting documents, with USCIS.

  • Wait for a Decision: USCIS reviews the application. If approved, the U Visa allows the victim to stay and work in the U.S. for up to four years.
