  • Honduras

    The youth in Honduras is lacking resurces to flourishh and live a better life. Due to lack of information, some are aware of their chance at the American dream and opportinities available. USAPathways contacted families in Honduras to educate them and shed light on a new path, offering guidance for their future

  • Camara Geutanne

    “After I arrived in the United States, I met many wonderful families that supported me and helped me establish myself in the country. The U.S. has become my home thanks to God, and these wonderful families. I was even able to open my own business. Since coming to the United States, many doors have opened for me and I have walked through these doors with my head held high. I thank God for putting Ms. Nelly and Ms. Jennifer on my path, they welcomed me with open arms and helped me make the US my home.”

  • Anne-Sophie
    School for Advanced Studies Class of 2023
    Williams College
    Class of 2027

    “The education I received was undoubtedly one of the most significant changes and opportunities I've had since coming to the United States. I left Haiti due to political and economic issues that affected the educational system. Although I arrived just before COVID-19, it was clear that the United States invests a lot more in education. They provided resources like laptops and ensured basic necessities such as WiFi and electricity were readily available for students. I am truly grateful to have been able to come to the United States, even during the pandemic, as I was able to study and learn English and much more. I was given the opportunity to take dual enrollment courses at School for Advanced Studies, participate in internships, and actively advocate for my district, opportunities not offered in other countries like Haiti. I am so glad to have immigrated to America for its benefits, opportunities, and the community I found.”

  • Walkiria Hernandez

    "I came to the United States at the end of 2014 with a work visa. Right from the start, I was aware that I would find better and more opportunities in the job market here than in my home country. I began as a regular employee, and within six months, I secured the position of department supervisor. After six years, I was promoted to the role of Manager at a pig farm. On a personal level, adapting to the system was crucial. For instance, understanding the concept of credit and how to build it, navigating the transportation system (handling licenses, plates, and insurance), and adhering to local regulations not only saved me from many problems but also allowed me to lead a more organized life. These adjustments come with several advantages, including the ease of purchasing my own home in a relatively short period, access to excellent educational opportunities for my children, and, most importantly, the chance to continue personal and professional growth. Age is often a limiting factor in Latin American countries, but here, there are always opportunities for self-improvement and pursuing new life projects."
